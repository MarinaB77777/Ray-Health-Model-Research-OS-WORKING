# Research Governance Contract v1

## Purpose

This document defines the governance principles of the research platform.

Governance protects scientific integrity.

Governance does not perform scientific analysis.

Governance does not create hypotheses.

Governance does not interpret results.

Governance verifies that research actions comply with platform rules.

---

# Core Principle

Scientific validity is determined by evidence.

Governance determines whether an action is permitted.

Governance never determines whether a hypothesis is true.

---

# Anti-Fabrication Scientific Rule

No data means no conclusion.

If data is missing, insufficient, contradictory, or unknown, the platform must explicitly mark this state.

The platform must never invent:

- data;
- mechanisms;
- dependencies;
- causal explanations;
- statistical results;
- validation results;
- participant traits;
- scientific conclusions.

If the system does not know, it must ask for clarification or return:

not_enough_data

unknown

needs_more_evidence

needs_human_review

---

# Hypothesis Evidence Rule

A hypothesis is not a conclusion.

A hypothesis may only be strengthened through:

- predefined scientific checks;
- statistical analysis;
- controlled comparison;
- replication;
- validation;
- experiment;
- evidence review.

No hypothesis may become accepted knowledge without evidence.

Ray-generated hypotheses remain Discovery objects until reviewed and validated.

---

# Research Integrity

The platform must preserve:

scientific transparency

reproducibility

traceability

version history

auditability

research independence

uncertainty reporting

negative results

failed replications

---

# Human Authority

The researcher has authority to:

create studies

define hypotheses

approve variables

approve Ray discoveries

define statistical plans

approve publications

archive studies

---

# Ray Authority

Ray may:

search for patterns

suggest variables

suggest hypotheses

suggest mechanisms

suggest statistical methods

identify inconsistencies

identify missing data

estimate sample requirements

propose validation studies

Ray may never silently modify human research.

---

# Validation Authority

Validation may:

confirm

weaken

reject

or leave unchanged

Research Objects.

Validation never rewrites historical analyses.

---

# Knowledge Authority

Only validated scientific knowledge may enter the Knowledge Base.

Candidate discoveries remain outside the Knowledge Base.

---

# Version Governance

Nothing is overwritten.

Every change creates a new version.

Historical studies remain reproducible forever.

---

# Audit Trail

Every research action records:

who

when

why

what changed

previous version

new version

approval status

---

# Human Override

Researchers may override Ray suggestions.

Researchers may reject Ray discoveries.

Researchers may ignore Ray recommendations.

All overrides remain recorded.

---

# Ray Transparency

Every Ray-generated object must state:

why it was generated

which variables were analyzed

which methods were used

what confidence exists

known limitations

why the suggestion was made

---

# Forbidden Actions

The platform must never:

silently modify datasets

silently modify hypotheses

silently delete historical results

silently replace statistical outputs

silently promote Ray discoveries

hide failed analyses

hide uncertainty

hide contradictory evidence

---

# Future Compatibility

Governance must remain compatible with:

Human Research

Ray Discovery

Validation Lab

Knowledge Base

External review

Scientific publication

Future regulatory requirements

without changing these principles.
