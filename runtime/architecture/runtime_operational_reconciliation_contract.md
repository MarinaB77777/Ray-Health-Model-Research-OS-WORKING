# Runtime Operational Reconciliation Definition

## Definition

Truth reconciliation is the bounded operational process of:
- comparing truth layers;
- evaluating contradictions;
- narrowing uncertainty where justified;
- preserving unresolved truth where necessary;
- updating scoped operational confidence;
- maintaining contradiction visibility.

Truth reconciliation may:
- reduce uncertainty;
- refine operational understanding;
- improve calibration;
- narrow operational ambiguity;
- update freshness-aware confidence.

Truth reconciliation is not:
- forced certainty creation;
- mandatory contradiction elimination;
- guaranteed convergence;
- omniscient reality synthesis;
- automatic truth collapse.

---

## Layered Reconciliation Boundary

Truth reconciliation must preserve truth-layer boundaries.

Core invariant:

truth in one layer  
must not silently collapse  
truth in another layer

Truth layers may include:
- execution truth;
- lifecycle truth;
- verification truth;
- human-confirmed truth;
- external reported truth;
- orchestration visibility truth;
- historical truth;
- unresolved truth.

Layer agreement alone must not silently create universal certainty.

---

## Cross-Layer Agreement Boundary

Agreement between layers is not omniscient certainty.

Core invariant:

agreement between layers  
≠ complete reality reconciliation

Aligned truth layers may still:
- share stale evidence;
- share hidden dependencies;
- share upstream errors;
- preserve visibility limitations;
- remain partially incomplete.

Runtime must preserve bounded humility regarding reconciled truth.

---

## Scope-Bounded Reconciliation

Truth reconciliation must remain bounded by scope.

Core invariant:

reconciliation in one scope  
≠ reconciliation in all scopes

Reconciliation in one operational scope must not silently:
- reconcile unrelated truth layers;
- reconcile unrelated lifecycle objects;
- reconcile unrelated execution state;
- reconcile unrelated human outcome semantics;
- reconcile unrelated dependency chains.

Scope boundaries must remain explicit.

---

## Probabilistic Reconciliation Boundary

Multiple weak reconciliations do not guarantee epistemic convergence.

multiple weak reconciliations

Repeated probabilistic agreement may still:

Probabilistic reconciliation is not verified certainty.

Core invariant:

probabilistic reconciliation  
≠ verified operational certainty

Probability must not silently become:

- complete operational certainty.

Runtime must preserve probability semantics explicitly.
