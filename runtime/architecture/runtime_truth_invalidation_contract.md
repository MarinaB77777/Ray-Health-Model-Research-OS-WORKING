## Runtime Truth Invalidation Definition

Truth invalidation is the bounded operational process of reducing, revoking, superseding, downgrading, or removing current operational validity from previously accepted truth.

Truth invalidation may occur due to:
- freshness expiration;
- contradiction;
- replay detection;
- scope invalidation;
- dependency change;
- operational context change;
- visibility reduction;
- uncertainty increase;
- source invalidation;
- verification downgrade;
- authority invalidation;
- external-state divergence;
- evidence decay.

Truth invalidation is not:
- historical erasure;
- audit deletion;
- architecture failure;
- proof that historical truth never existed.

---

## Invalidation Is Not Deletion

Truth invalidation does not erase historical existence.

Core invariant:

truth invalidation  
≠ truth deletion

Invalidated truth may remain:
- historically true;
- audit-relevant;
- reviewable;
- explainable;
- contextually useful;
- part of downgrade history.

Invalidation removes or downgrades current operational validity.

It does not erase historical trace.
