## Uncertainty Definition

Uncertainty is a legitimate operational condition where available information is:
- incomplete;
- partial;
- stale;
- probabilistic;
- contradictory;
- scope-limited;
- visibility-limited;
- confidence-limited;
- freshness-limited;
- source-limited.

Uncertainty may apply to:
- facts;
- intent;
- risk;
- forecast;
- execution result;
- verification;
- lifecycle state;
- human outcome;
- external state;
- projection-informed reasoning;
- pattern interpretation.

Uncertainty is not:
- failure;
- incompetence;
- uselessness;
- reason to fabricate certainty;
- reason to hide limitations.
